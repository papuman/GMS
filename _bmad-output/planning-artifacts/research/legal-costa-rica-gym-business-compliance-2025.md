## 1. Labor Laws for Gym Staff

### Primary Legal Framework
**Governing Law:** Código de Trabajo (Labor Code - Law No. 2 of 1943)
**Official Source:** [Sistema Costarricense de Información Jurídica](https://pgrweb.go.cr/scij/Busqueda/Normativa/Normas/nrm_texto_completo.aspx?param1=NRTC&nValor1=1&nValor2=8045&nValor3=101952&strTipM=TC)

### 1.1 Employment Contracts

**Legal Requirements:**
- Written employment contracts are mandatory for all full-time employees
- Verbal contracts only permitted for short-term agricultural or temporary work
- Contracts must specify: position, salary, working hours, start date, benefits
- Article 18 presumes employment relationship from mere initiation of work

**Contract Types:**
- **Indefinite contracts:** Standard for permanent positions (most common)
- **Fixed-term contracts:** Only for temporary positions with known end date

**Sources:**
- [Costa Rica Employment Law Guide](https://www.bizlatinhub.com/employment-law-costa-rica-guide/)
- [Labor Code Overview](https://www.safeguardglobal.com/country/costa-rica/employment-law/)

### 1.2 Working Hours and Shifts

**Regulatory Articles:** Labor Code Articles 136, 139, 143
**Constitutional Basis:** Article 58 of Costa Rican Constitution

**Maximum Working Hours:**
- **Day shift (5:00 AM - 7:00 PM):** 8 hours/day, 48 hours/week
- **Night shift (7:00 PM - 5:00 AM):** 6 hours/day, 36 hours/week
- **Mixed shift:** 7 hours/day, 42 hours/week

**Overtime Regulations:**
- Anything exceeding 48 hours/week = overtime
- Maximum 4 overtime hours per day (12-hour shift limit)
- **Overtime rate:** 150% of regular wages (time and a half)
- Overtime must be exceptional, temporary, and for unforeseen tasks

**Exemptions:**
- Managers, administrators, employees of trust (Article 143)
- Cannot exceed 12 hours daily
- Entitled to minimum 1.5 hours rest

**Sources:**
- [Labor Code - pgrweb.go.cr](https://pgrweb.go.cr/scij/Busqueda/Normativa/Normas/nrm_texto_completo.aspx?param1=NRTC&nValor1=1&nValor2=8045&nValor3=101952&strTipM=TC)
- [Employment Laws Overview](https://www.skuad.io/employment-laws/costa-rica)

### 1.3 Minimum Wage (2025)

**Regulatory Body:** Ministerio de Trabajo y Seguridad Social (MTSS)

**2025 Minimum Wage Rates (daily):**
- Unskilled workers: ₡12,236.95/day
- Specialized workers: ₡15,983.95/day
- Rates vary by skill level and occupation category

**Important:** Minimum wage increases annually; verify current rates with MTSS

**Sources:**
- [2025 Employment Law Guide](https://www.bizlatinhub.com/employment-law-costa-rica-guide/)
- [Salary Increase Information](https://www.jadelrio.com/cr/en/blogs/salary-increase-of-1-63-effective-as-of-january-2026)

### 1.4 Mandatory Employee Benefits

#### 1.4.1 Social Security (CCSS)
**Governing Body:** Caja Costarricense de Seguro Social
**Legal Requirement:** All employers must register and contribute

**2025 Contribution Rates:**
- **Employer:** 26.67% of gross salary
- **Employee:** 10.67% of gross salary

**Breakdown of Employer Contributions (26.67% total):**
- Health Insurance (SEM): 9.25%
- Pension (IVM): 5.42% (increases to 5.58% in January 2026)
- INA (National Training Institute): Included
- FODESAF (Family Allowance Fund): Included
- Banco Popular: Included
- FCL (Housing/Severance Fund): 1.5%

**2026 Update:**
- Effective January 1, 2026: Employer IVM increases from 5.42% to 5.58%
- Employee IVM increases from 4.17% to 4.33%

**Sources:**
- [2025 CCSS Contribution Rates](https://crie.cr/rates-for-caja-ccss-contributions-in-costa-rica/)
- [BDO 2026 Adjustments](https://www.bdo.cr/en-gb/insights/2025/costa-rica-adjustment-to-ccss-employer-employee-contributions-effective-january-2026)
- [CCSS Employer Obligations](https://costaricalaw.com/costa-rica-legal-topics/labor-law/employer-obligations-for-employee-insurance-with-the-costa-rican-social-security-administration-ccss/)

#### 1.4.2 Aguinaldo (13th Month Salary)
**Legal Basis:** Labor Code mandatory benefit
**Payment Deadline:** December 20 (no exceptions)

**Calculation Formula:**
```
Total salary-type earnings (Dec 1 - Nov 30) ÷ 12 = Aguinaldo
```

**What's Included:**
- Base salary
- Overtime pay
- Commissions
- Holiday pay
- Salary in kind (benefits, goods, services)
- Maternity/paternity/adoption leave payments

**What's Excluded:**
- Disability subsidy payments

**Tax Treatment:**
- 100% exempt from income tax
- 100% exempt from CCSS deductions
- Only deduction allowed: court-ordered child support

**Sources:**
- [Christmas Bonus Guide 2025](https://aglegal.com/some-important-aspects-about-the-christmas-bonus/)
- [Aguinaldo Complete Guide](https://crie.cr/what-is-the-aguinaldo-in-costa-rica-annual-employee-bonus-guide/)
- [Understanding Aguinaldo](https://qcostarica.com/understanding-the-aguinaldo-costa-ricas-year-end-bonus-and-how-to-calculate-it/)

#### 1.4.3 Vacation Days (Vacaciones)
**Legal Entitlement:** 2 weeks paid vacation per 50 weeks of continuous work

**Calculation:**
- Vacation wages = average weekly wage over previous 50 weeks
- If terminated before 1 year: 1 day wages per month worked

**Accrual:**
- Begins after 50 consecutive weeks of service
- Unused vacation must be paid upon termination

**Sources:**
- [Costa Rica Vacation Policy](https://www.costaricalaborlawyer.com/vacationandholidays.html)
- [Leave Policy Guide](https://www.skuad.io/leave-policy/costa-rica)

#### 1.4.4 Maternity Leave
**Legal Provision:** 4 months (120 days) total leave
**Distribution:** 1 month before due date + 3 months after birth

**Payment Structure:**
- 50% paid by CCSS
- 50% paid by employer
- Total: 100% salary coverage

**Special Circumstances:**
- Complicated pregnancy/termination: 2 months leave
- Health complications: up to 3 months leave

**Job Protection:**
- Cannot be terminated during maternity leave
- Protection against pregnancy-based employment decisions
- Prohibition on requiring pregnancy tests for employment

**Additional Rights:**
- **Breastfeeding breaks:** 1 hour paid time (start/end of day) OR 15-min every 3 hours OR two 30-min breaks
- **Lactation room:** Employer must provide
- **Medical appointments:** Up to 1 day paid leave for pregnancy certificates and child medical appointments

**Sources:**
- [Maternity Leave Guide](https://www.bizlatinhub.com/employment-law-costa-rica-guide/)
- [Leave Rights in Costa Rica](https://rivermate.com/guides/costa-rica/leave)
- [2022 Labor Code Amendments](https://www.dentons.com/en/insights/articles/2024/march/25/costa-rica-updates-on-the-special-paternity-leave-in-the-labor-code)

#### 1.4.5 Paternity Leave
**Legal Provision:** 8 days total
**Distribution:** 2 days per week during first 4 weeks of child's life

**Payment Structure:**
- 50% paid by CCSS
- 50% paid by employer
- Total: 100% salary coverage

**Effective Date:** June 3, 2022 (Labor Code amendments)

**Sources:**
- [Paternity Leave Updates](https://www.dentons.com/en/insights/articles/2024/march/25/costa-rica-updates-on-the-special-paternity-leave-in-the-labor-code)
- [2025 Leave Policy](https://www.skuad.io/leave-policy/costa-rica)

#### 1.4.6 Adoption Leave
**Entitlement:** 3 months paid leave
**Start Date:** Day after child placement
**Coverage:** Joint entitlement for working couples (simultaneous or consecutive)

**Sources:**
- [Employee Leave Rights](https://rivermate.com/guides/costa-rica/leave)

### 1.5 Employment Termination

#### 1.5.1 Notice Period (Preaviso)
**Applies After:** 3 months of employment

**Notice Requirements by Length of Service:**
- 3-6 months: 1 week notice
- 6 months-1 year: 15 days notice
- More than 1 year: 1 month notice

**Cash Payment Alternative:**
- Based on average salary from last 6 months
- Must provide written notice if given as time
- During notice period: 1 day off per week to find new job

**Not Required When:**
- Employee quits voluntarily
- Employee terminated with just cause

**Sources:**
- [Notice and Termination](https://www.costaricalaborlawyer.com/noticeandterminationpay.html)
- [Termination Guide](https://rivermate.com/guides/costa-rica/termination)

#### 1.5.2 Severance Pay (Cesantía)
**Legal Basis:** Mandatory for termination without just cause
**Maximum Coverage:** Last 8 years of employment

**Calculation Formula:**
```
(Average monthly salary over last 6 months OR last month, whichever is higher)
× (Number of years of service, capped at 8 years)
```

**Scale by Length of Service:**
- 3-6 months: 7 days wages
- 6 months-1 year: 14 days wages
- 1+ years: Proportional calculation up to 8 years max

**Tax Treatment:**
- No social security deductions apply to severance

**Not Required When:**
- Termination with just cause
- Employee voluntary resignation

**Sources:**
- [Severance Pay Guide](https://news.outlierlegal.com/2021/07/05/what-you-should-know-about-severance-pay-in-costa-rica/)
- [Severance Calculation](https://www.bufetegodinezyasociados.com/en-us/Blog/Articulo/84/TheSeveranceAndItsCalculation)
- [Severance Rules](https://www.playroll.com/compliance-hub/costa-rica)

#### 1.5.3 Just Cause Termination (Sin Responsabilidad Patronal)
**Legal Basis:** Article 81, Costa Rica Labor Code

**Categories of Just Cause:**

**a) Conduct During Work Hours:**
- Openly immoral behavior
- Insult, slander, or physical violence against employer
- Similar acts against coworkers that disrupt discipline/work

**b) Other Just Causes:**
- Legal troubles
- Deliberate misleading (false qualifications, resume fraud)
- Breaking confidentiality agreements
- Violations of employment contract clauses

**Procedural Requirements:**
- Employer must prove existence of just cause
- Termination letter must specify exact reasons
- Procedural fairness required even with clear infraction
- Failure to properly inform = termination without just cause

**Employer Obligations When Terminated With Cause:**
- Aguinaldo (13th month) payment: YES
- Vacation payment: YES
- Severance (cesantía): NO
- Notice (preaviso): NO

**Sources:**
- [Just Cause Dismissal](https://salasegunda.poder-judicial.go.cr/revista/Revista_N13/contenido/PDFs/art-01.pdf)
- [Dismissal Grounds](https://www.brr-law.legal/blog/causales-de-despidos-en-costa-rica/)
- [Termination Guide](https://rivermate.com/guides/costa-rica/termination)

### 1.6 Additional Labor Funds

#### FCL (Fondo de Capitalización Laboral)
**Purpose:** Labor Capitalization/Housing Fund
**Employer Contribution:** 1.5% of salary reported to CCSS
**Management:** Pension operators
**Withdrawal:** Every 5 years if continuously employed with same employer

#### Pension Funds (ROP/OPC)
**Full Name:** Régimen Obligatorio de Pensiones Complementarias
**Purpose:** Mandatory complementary pension system
**Management:** Operadoras de Pensiones Complementarias (OPCs)
**Contributions:** Both employer and employee contribute

**Sources:**
- [Employee Benefits Overview](https://rivermate.com/guides/costa-rica/benefits)

## 2. Data Retention and Privacy Laws

### Primary Legal Framework
**Governing Law:** Ley 8968 - Ley de Protección de la Persona frente al tratamiento de sus datos personales
**Publication:** La Gaceta No. 170, September 5, 2011
**Regulation:** Decree N° 37554-JP
**Enforcement Agency:** PRODHAB (Agencia de Protección de Datos de los Habitantes)

**Official Sources:**
- [Law 8968 - MICITT](https://micitt.go.cr/node/370)
- [Legal Text - SCIJ](https://pgrweb.go.cr/scij/Busqueda/Normativa/Normas/nrm_texto_completo.aspx?param1=NRTC&nValor1=1&nValor2=70975&nValor3=85989&strTipM=TC)
- [Regulation - SCIJ](https://pgrweb.go.cr/scij/Busqueda/Normativa/Normas/nrm_texto_completo.aspx?param1=NRTC&nValor1=1&nValor2=74352&nValor3=106487&strTipM=TC)

### 2.1 Scope and Objective

**Who is Protected:**
- Any person, regardless of nationality, residence, or domicile

**What is Protected:**
- Right to informational self-determination (Article 4)
- Respect for private life and personality rights
- Freedom and equality regarding automated or manual data processing

**Sources:**
- [Data Protection Overview](https://www.dataguidance.com/notes/costa-rica-data-protection-overview)
- [DLA Piper Guide](https://www.dlapiperdataprotection.com/index.html?t=law&c=CR)

### 2.2 PRODHAB (Enforcement Agency)

**Full Name:** Agencia de Protección de Datos de los Habitantes
**Structure:** Independent body under Ministry of Justice and Peace

**Powers and Responsibilities:**
- Create awareness of data protection rights
- Inspect for compliance
- Order deletion, rectification, or correction of data
- Impose administrative and economic sanctions
- Act ex officio or at request of interested party

**Sources:**
- [PRODHAB Overview](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/data-privacy-regulations-compliance-costa-rica-arturo-rojas)
- [Data Protection Guide](https://multilaw.com/Multilaw/Multilaw/Data_Protection_Laws_Guide/DataProtection_Guide_Costa_Rica.aspx)

### 2.3 Database Registration Requirements

#### Who Must Register:
- **Public or private databases** managed for distribution or marketing purposes
- **Exempt:** Internal-use databases (not for distribution/marketing)

#### Registration Process:
**Required Information:**
- Signed registration request
- Designation of responsible persons
- Details of security measures for data treatment
- Recipients of the data
- Copy of existing acting protocols
- Method of service
- Provision of "superuser" account for database (even if maintained by service provider)

**Annual Fee:** $200 USD

**Applicable to Gyms:**
- Member databases used for marketing: **MUST REGISTER**
- Internal member management only: **EXEMPT**

**Sources:**
- [Registration Requirements](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/data-privacy-regulations-compliance-costa-rica-arturo-rojas)
- [Data Protection Guide](https://multilaw.com/Multilaw/Multilaw/Data_Protection_Laws_Guide/DataProtection_Guide_Costa_Rica.aspx)

### 2.4 Data Breach Notification

**Timeline:** 5 business days after breach discovery

**Who to Notify:**
1. PRODHAB (regulatory agency)
2. Affected data subjects

**Required Information:**
- Nature of the breach
- Compromised personal data categories
- Immediate corrective actions taken
- Additional preventive and corrective measures
- Contact information for further inquiries

**Consequences of Non-Compliance:**
- Potential fines from PRODHAB
- Administrative sanctions

**Sources:**
- [Breach Notification Requirements](https://www.hunton.com/privacy-and-information-security-law/costa-rica-imposes-five-day-breach-notification-obligation-and-other-stringent-data-protection-requirement)
- [Security Requirements](https://www.dlapiperdataprotection.com/index.html?t=security&c=CR)

### 2.5 Penalties and Sanctions

**Range of Penalties:**
- **Economic sanctions:** 5 to 30 base salaries (approximately $4,000 to $24,000 USD)
- **Alternative amounts cited:** $3,000 to $18,000 USD
- **Severe cases:** Discontinuation of database use for 1-6 months

**When Applied:**
- Non-compliance with data protection requirements
- Data breaches
- Failure to register databases
- Violation of data subject rights

**Sources:**
- [Penalties Overview](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/data-privacy-regulations-compliance-costa-rica-arturo-rojas)
- [Compliance Guide](https://multilaw.com/Multilaw/Multilaw/Data_Protection_Laws_Guide/DataProtection_Guide_Costa_Rica.aspx)

### 2.6 Implementation Status

**Important Note:**
- Law 8968 has been criticized for lacking strict enforcement
- Compliance reported to be extremely low in both government and private sector
- Law considered outdated (issued 2011)
- Two draft laws in legislative pipeline to modernize framework

**Recommendation:** Despite low historical enforcement, compliance is legally required and recommended to avoid future penalties as enforcement increases.

**Sources:**
- [Implementation Analysis](https://caseguard.com/articles/law-no-8968-of-2011-costa-rican-data-rights/)
- [Enforcement Status](https://www.dataguidance.com/jurisdiction/costa-rica)

### 2.7 Actionable Requirements for Gyms

**For Marketing/Commercial Databases:**
1. Register database with PRODHAB
2. Pay $200 annual fee
3. Implement security measures for data treatment
4. Designate data protection responsible person
5. Create breach response protocol (5-day notification)
6. Maintain superuser access for PRODHAB inspection

**For Internal-Only Databases:**
1. Ensure data only used internally
2. Document that database is not used for distribution/marketing
3. Implement reasonable security measures
4. Prepare breach notification protocol

**Best Practices:**
- Obtain written consent for data collection
- Provide privacy policy to members
- Allow members to access, correct, and delete their data
- Implement data minimization (only collect necessary data)
- Secure storage with encryption where possible
- Train staff on data protection requirements

## 3. Consumer Protection Laws

### Primary Legal Framework
**Governing Law:** Ley 7472 - Ley de Promoción de la Competencia y Defensa Efectiva del Consumidor
**Enforcement Agency:** Comisión Nacional del Consumidor (CNC) - National Consumer Commission
**Parent Ministry:** MEIC - Ministerio de Economía, Industria y Comercio

**Official Sources:**
- [Consumer Protection Law](https://costaricalaw.com/costa-rica-legal-topics/consumer-protection-laws-in-costa-rica/consumer-protection-law-7472/)
- [MEIC Information](https://www.lexmundi.com/guides/latam-consumer-guide-2024/jurisdiction/latin-america-caribbean/costa-rica/)

### 3.1 Consumer Rights Under Law 7472

**Protected Rights:**
1. Protection against health and safety risks
2. Protection of economic and social interests
3. Access to accurate and timely information on products/services
4. Education on appropriate consumption of goods/services
5. Administrative and judicial protection against misleading advertising
6. Protection against abusive practices and clauses
7. Protection against unfair commercial methods restricting free choice

**Sources:**
- [Consumer Rights Overview](https://garrolaw.com/learn-about-consumer-protection-laws-in-costa-rica/)
- [Consumer Protection Guide](https://www.lexmundi.com/guides/latam-consumer-guide-2024/jurisdiction/latin-america-caribbean/costa-rica/)

### 3.2 Adhesion Contracts (Gym Memberships)

**Definition:** Pre-drafted contracts where consumer has no ability to negotiate terms

**2023 Amendments (Bill 22.901):**
- Effective March 30, 2023
- Title: "Access to Justice for Consumers"
- **Key Change:** National Consumer Commission can declare nullity of abusive clauses in adhesion contracts
- **Exception:** Financial contracts excluded

**Applicable to Gyms:** YES - Gym membership contracts are adhesion contracts

**Sources:**
- [2023 Amendments](https://consortiumlegal.com/en/2023/04/20/costa-rica-amendments-to-law-7472-law-for-the-promotion-of-competition-and-effective-defense-of-the-consumer/)
- [Adhesion Contracts](https://costaricalaw.com/costa-rica-legal-topics/consumer-protection-laws-in-costa-rica/costa-rica-adhesion-contracts-and-consumer-rights/)

### 3.3 Abusive Clauses

**Definition:** Contract clauses that unfairly disadvantage consumers

**National Consumer Commission Authority:**
- Can declare abusive clauses null and void
- Acts as Administrative Court
- No court filing required for initial complaint

**Examples of Potentially Abusive Clauses in Gym Contracts:**
- Excessive cancellation penalties
- Automatic renewal without clear notice
- Unilateral contract modification rights
- Waiver of liability for negligence
- Restriction of legal remedies

**Sources:**
- [Abusive Clauses Authority](https://consortiumlegal.com/en/2023/04/20/costa-rica-amendments-to-law-7472-law-for-the-promotion-of-competition-and-effective-defense-of-the-consumer/)
- [Consumer Protection Elements](https://www.theworldlawgroup.com/membership/news/news-elements-of-consumer-protection-in-the-context-of-traditional-and-electronic-commerce-in-costa-rica)

### 3.4 Right of Withdrawal

**Consumer Right:** Ability to render concluded contract ineffective
**Requirements:**
- Notification within established period
- No justification required
- No penalty for exercising right

**Null and Void:** Any clauses imposing penalties for exercising withdrawal right

**Application to Gyms:**
- Gyms should provide clear withdrawal/cancellation policy
- Penalties for withdrawal may be challenged as abusive

**Sources:**
- [Right of Withdrawal](https://www.theworldlawgroup.com/membership/news/news-elements-of-consumer-protection-in-the-context-of-traditional-and-electronic-commerce-in-costa-rica)

### 3.5 Liability for Defective Services (Article 35)

**Legal Standard:** Strict liability regardless of fault

**Article 35 Provision:**
"Producer, supplier and merchant must respond concurrently and independently of the existence of fault, if the consumer is harmed by reason of the good or service, inadequate or insufficient information about them or their use and risks."

**Applicable to Gyms:**
- Liability for equipment defects causing injury
- Liability for inadequate safety information
- Liability for insufficient instruction on equipment use
- Concurrent liability (gym + equipment manufacturer + service provider)

**Sources:**
- [Consumer Protection Guide](https://www.lexmundi.com/guides/latam-consumer-guide-2024/jurisdiction/latin-america-caribbean/costa-rica/)
- [Article 35 Analysis](https://www.theworldlawgroup.com/membership/news/news-elements-of-consumer-protection-in-the-context-of-traditional-and-electronic-commerce-in-costa-rica)

### 3.6 Consumer Complaint Process

**Available Remedies:**
1. Return of money or product
2. Repair or replacement of goods
3. Declaration of nullity of abusive clauses
4. Imposition of fines on businesses

**Where to File:**
- National Consumer Commission (CNC)
- Decentralized entity of MEIC

**Process:**
- Administrative resolution (no court required initially)
- CNC acts as Administrative Court
- Decisions subject to judicial review

**Sources:**
- [Complaint Process](https://www.lexmundi.com/guides/latam-consumer-guide-2024/jurisdiction/latin-america-caribbean/costa-rica/)
- [CNC Authority](https://consortiumlegal.com/en/2023/04/20/costa-rica-amendments-to-law-7472-law-for-the-promotion-of-competition-and-effective-defense-of-the-consumer/)

### 3.7 Actionable Requirements for Gym Membership Contracts

**Contract Transparency:**
1. Provide clear, readable contract terms
2. Highlight key terms (duration, price, cancellation policy)
3. Provide copy of contract before and after signing
4. Ensure consumer has opportunity to review before signing

**Fair Cancellation Policy:**
1. Establish reasonable cancellation procedures
2. Avoid excessive penalties
3. Provide clear withdrawal rights
4. Allow cancellation for relocation or medical reasons

**Information Disclosure:**
1. Clearly explain all fees (membership, enrollment, processing)
2. Disclose automatic renewal terms
3. Provide safety information for equipment
4. Explain risks and proper use of facilities

**Prohibited Practices:**
1. Hidden fees not disclosed upfront
2. Automatic renewal without clear advance notice
3. Making cancellation unreasonably difficult
4. Retaining payment for services not provided
5. Waiving liability for gross negligence

**Documentation:**
1. Maintain records of all consumer contracts
2. Document consent for automatic renewals
3. Keep cancellation requests with processing dates
4. Maintain evidence of information disclosure

## 4. Health and Safety Regulations for Fitness Facilities

### Primary Legal Framework
**Governing Authority:** Ministerio de Salud (Ministry of Health)
**Key Regulation:** Manual de Normas para la Habilitación de Centros de Acondicionamiento Físico (2006)
**Current Guidelines:** LS-CS-006 Lineamientos para funcionamiento de centros o espacios de acondicionamiento físico

**Official Sources:**
- [Ministry of Health Website](https://www.ministeriodesalud.go.cr)
- [Fitness Center Guidelines Version 8](https://www.ministeriodesalud.go.cr/index.php/biblioteca-de-archivos-left/documentos-ministerio-de-salud/vigilancia-de-la-salud/normas-protocolos-guias-y-lineamientos/situacion-nacional-covid-19/lineamientos-especificos-covid-19/historico-lineamientos-covid-19/5449-version-8-25-de-mayo-2022-lineamientos-para-funcionamiento-de-centros-o-espacios-de-acondicionamiento-fisico-para-la-realizacion-de-actividad-fisica-sin-contacto-fisico/file)

### 4.1 Fitness Center Definition

**Legal Definition:**
Establishments whose function relates to physical exercise, health, sports, rehabilitation and recreation, executing activities related to training in individual and collective exercise plans and programs, whose purpose is to improve and maintain clients' physical fitness.

**Applicable Facilities:**
- Traditional gyms
- CrossFit facilities
- Functional training spaces
- Physical conditioning centers
- Rehabilitation centers with fitness programs

**Sources:**
- [Manual de Normas](https://vlex.co.cr/vid/manual-normas-centros-sico-485037930)
- [Fitness Center Standards](http://www.pgrweb.go.cr/scij/Busqueda/Normativa/Normas/nrm_texto_completo.aspx?param1=NRTC&nValor1=1&nValor2=58901&nValor3=65562&strTipM=TC)

### 4.2 Permiso Sanitario de Funcionamiento (Sanitary Operating Permit)

**Requirement:** MANDATORY for all fitness facilities
**Governing Decree:** Decree 43432-S
**Validity Period:** 5 years

#### 4.2.1 Risk Classification

**Three Risk Groups:**
- **Group A (High Risk):** ₡55,000 fee
- **Group B (Moderate Risk):** ₡27,500 fee
- **Group C (Low Risk):** ₡16,500 fee

**Note:** Specific classification for gyms found in Annex 1 of Decree 43432-S

#### 4.2.2 Pre-Conditions for Application

**Before Submitting Application:**
1. Current and up-to-date CCSS registration (as employer, independent worker, or both)
2. No delinquency with tax obligations to Ministry of Finance
3. Current with payment of any Ministry of Health fines

## 6. Insurance and Liability Requirements

### 6.1 Mandatory Workers' Compensation Insurance

**Legal Name:** Seguro de Riesgos del Trabajo
**Legal Requirement:** MANDATORY for all employers
**Provider:** Instituto Nacional de Seguros (INS) - government monopoly
**Penalty for Non-Compliance:** Personal liability for all medical expenses from work-related injuries

**Official Source:** [INS Riesgos del Trabajo](https://www.grupoins.com/ins-pyme/pyme-seguros/riesgos-del-trabajo/)

#### 6.1.1 Coverage

**What's Covered:**
- Work-related injuries
- Work-related accidents
- Occupational diseases
- Medical expenses
- Disability benefits
- Rehabilitation costs

**Who Must Be Covered:**
- ALL employees
- No exceptions based on hours worked or contract type

#### 6.1.2 Premium Rates

**Variable Rates by Occupation:**
- Office employees: 0.57% of wages
- Physical labor: Higher percentage (varies by risk classification)
- **Gym staff:** Likely higher than office rate due to physical nature and injury risk

**Payment Options:**
- Annual (most common)
- Quarterly
- Twice yearly

**Calculation Base:** Total employee wages reported to CCSS

#### 6.1.3 Consequences of Non-Compliance

**Without Insurance:**
- Employer personally liable for ALL medical costs
- Employer liable for rehabilitation expenses
- Employer liable for disability payments
- Potential criminal penalties
- Cannot obtain other required business permits

**Sources:**
- [Workers' Compensation Guide](https://costaricalaw.com/costa-rica-legal-topics/insurance-law/understanding-mandatory-workers-compensation-insurance-in-costa-rica-a-comprehensive-guide/)
- [Labor Law Overview](https://costaricalaw.com/costa-rica-legal-topics/labor-law/labor-law-in-a-nutshell/)

#### 6.1.4 Management Platform

**RT Virtual Platform:**
- Online management of Riesgos del Trabajo insurance
- Submit payroll updates
- Provisional inclusion of employees
- File accident notifications
- Manage policy administration

**Access:** Available through INS website

**Sources:**
- [INS Platform](https://www.grupoins.com/ins-pyme/pyme-seguros/riesgos-del-trabajo/)

### 6.2 Recommended Additional Insurance

**While not legally mandatory, the following insurance types are highly recommended for gym operations:**

#### 6.2.1 General Liability Insurance
**Coverage:**
- Third-party bodily injury
- Property damage
- Personal injury claims
- Legal defense costs

**Why Needed:**
- Member injuries from equipment
- Slip and fall accidents
- Damage to members' personal property

#### 6.2.2 Professional Liability Insurance
**Coverage:**
- Errors and omissions by trainers
- Negligent instruction
- Inadequate supervision claims

**Who Needs It:**
- Personal trainers
- Group fitness instructors
- Nutritionists (if providing nutrition services)

#### 6.2.3 Property Insurance
**Coverage:**
- Building damage (if owned)
- Equipment damage/theft
- Business interruption
- Fire, flood, earthquake

**Why Needed:**
- Protect expensive fitness equipment investment

#### 6.2.4 Cyber Insurance
**Coverage:**

- Legal notification expenses

**Why Needed:**

### 6.3 Liability Waivers and Risk Management

**Legal Limitation:**

**Recommended Approach:**

## 7. Accessibility Requirements

### Primary Legal Framework
**Governing Law:** Ley 7600 - Ley de Igualdad de Oportunidades para las Personas con Discapacidad
**Enacted:** May 1996
**Inspiration:** Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990
**Enforcement Agency:** CONAPDIS (Consejo Nacional de Personas con Discapacidad)

**Official Sources:**
- [Ley 7600 Full Text](https://pgrweb.go.cr/scij/Busqueda/Normativa/Normas/nrm_texto_completo.aspx?param1=NRTC&nValor1=1&nValor2=23261&nValor3=96047)
- [Law and Regulations - CONAPDIS](https://conapdis.go.cr/download/ley-7600-y-su-reglamento/)
- [Overview Guide](https://costaricalaw.com/costa-rica-medical-and-healthcare/overview-of-costa-ricas-disability-law-7600/)

### 7.1 Scope and Purpose

**Who is Protected:**
- All persons with disabilities
- Regardless of disability type (physical, sensory, cognitive)

**What is Guaranteed:**
- Equal opportunities in all areas of life
- Access to public and private institutions
- Physical environment accessibility
- Transportation access
- Information and communication access
- Participation in society, education, and work

**Applicable Establishments:**
- ALL public facilities
- ALL private commercial establishments (including gyms)
- New construction AND existing buildings

**Sources:**
- [Disability Law Overview](https://blog.nativu.com/en/what-is-law-7600-in-costa-rica-main-features/)
- [WHO Disability Legislation](https://extranet.who.int/mindbank/collection/country/costa_rica/disability_legislation)

### 7.2 Architectural Accessibility Requirements

**Key Principle:** Public and private buildings for collective use must have accessible infrastructure

#### 7.2.1 Required Accessibility Features

**Entrances and Exits:**
- Ramps with appropriate slope
- Handrails on both sides
- Non-slip surfaces
- Width adequate for wheelchair passage

**Circulation:**
- Hallways minimum 1.20 m width (consistent with health requirements)
- No architectural barriers
- Clear signage
- Adequate lighting

**Elevators/Platforms:**
- Required for multi-story facilities
- Platform elevators where full elevators not feasible
- Controls at wheelchair-accessible height

**Parking:**
- Reserved accessible parking spaces
- Clearly marked with international symbol
- Located near main entrance
- Appropriate dimensions for wheelchair access

**Restrooms:**
- At least one accessible restroom
- Grab bars installed
- Adequate turning radius for wheelchairs
- Accessible sinks and fixtures

**Signage:**
- Visual signage with high contrast
- Tactile signage where appropriate
- Braille signage for critical information
- International symbols for accessibility

**Sources:**
- [Ley 7600 Accessibility](https://www.elevadorescentroamericanos.com/ley-7600-accesibilidad/)
- [Architectural Requirements](https://blog.nativu.com/que-es-la-ley-7600-en-costa-rica-principales-caracteristicas/)

### 7.3 Information Accessibility

**Requirement:** All information for public use must be accessible according to persons' particular needs

**Application to Gyms:**
- Membership contracts in accessible formats
- Class schedules in large print/electronic format
- Safety instructions with visual aids
- Equipment instructions in multiple formats
- Website accessibility (screen reader compatible)

**Sources:**
- [Information Access Requirements](https://blog.nativu.com/en/what-is-law-7600-in-costa-rica-main-features/)

### 7.4 Employment Requirements

**Prohibition:** Discrimination against persons with disabilities in employment

**Application to Gyms:**
- Cannot refuse employment based solely on disability
- Must provide reasonable accommodations
- Accessible workplace for employees with disabilities
- Equal opportunity for advancement

### 7.5 Enforcement and Penalties

#### 7.5.1 Enforcement Mechanism

**Upon Victim Request:**
- Constructors/owners obliged to perform necessary adaptations
- Adaptations made at constructor/owner expense
- Ensures accessibility right

#### 7.5.2 Sanctions

**Challenge:** Few penalties for non-compliance in original law
**Result:** Key provisions often unenforced due to lack of sanctions

**Municipal Enforcement:**
- Some municipalities require Ley 7600 compliance for business licenses
- Liquor license renewals may require accessibility compliance
- Building permits require accessibility plans

**Consequences:**
- Difficulty obtaining business permits
- Legal difficulties
- Potential closure of establishment
- Economic sanctions (in some municipalities)

**Sources:**
- [Enforcement Challenges](https://ticotimes.net/2006/05/26/access-for-disabled-lags-despite-law)
- [Municipal Requirements](https://actualidadjudicial.poder-judicial.go.cr/vol92/noticias_prensa/np17.htm)

### 7.6 Implementation Reality

**Official Policy vs. Practice:**
- Laws based on international standards exist
- Lack of funding leads to uneven implementation
- 55.4% of educational centers not accessible (indicator of broader issue)
- Enforcement improving in recent years, especially for new construction

**Recommendation:** Full compliance advised despite historical weak enforcement to:
1. Avoid future penalties as enforcement increases
2. Expand potential customer base
3. Demonstrate social responsibility
4. Ensure municipal permit approval

**Sources:**
- [Implementation Status](https://www.angloinfo.com/how-to/costa-rica/healthcare/people-with-disabilities)
- [Disability Access Reality](https://www.expatfocus.com/costa-rica/guide/costa-rica-disability)

### 7.7 Actionable Accessibility Compliance

#### New Construction/Major Renovation:
- [ ] Include accessibility plan in building permit application
- [ ] Design ramps with proper slope (typically 1:12 ratio)
- [ ] Install handrails on all ramps and stairs
