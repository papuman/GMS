## SECTION 5: REGULATORY COMPLIANCE NIGHTMARE (SEPTEMBER 2025 CRISIS)

### SINPE Móvil Invoicing Mandate - NEW PAIN POINT

**Effective Date:** September 1, 2025 (4 months ago as of this research)

**What Changed:**
"Starting September 1, 2025, all businesses in Costa Rica that receive payments through SINPE Móvil must issue electronic invoices registering this payment method with code '06'."

This is **version 4.4** of Costa Rica's electronic invoicing requirements.

### Why This Crushes Small Gym Owners

**Before September 2025:**
- Gym owner receives SINPE Móvil payment from member
- Issues generic electronic invoice (if at all)

**After September 2025:**
- Must use specific code "06" for each SINPE payment
- Must cross-reference payment method in Hacienda system
- Invoice must be sent to Hacienda in real-time
- Failure results in penalties up to 150% of undeclared taxes

### Official Statement
**Ministry of Finance:**
"There will be no additional charges for registering SINPE Móvil payments in invoices. It is an administrative and formal requirement, not a new tax or fee."

**Translation:** It's not a new tax, but it IS new administrative work that small gym owners must handle.

### Penalties for Non-Compliance
**Confirmed penalties include:**
- Tax assessments for unreported income (VAT and income tax)
- Fines reaching "up to 150% of undeclared tax amounts"
- Preventive measures including bank account freezes
- Criminal proceedings for severe fraud cases

### Business Impact Described
"Businesses receiving payments via SINPE Móvil must register these transactions in their electronic invoices according to version 4.4 of the electronic invoicing system."

"Businesses that receive many SINPE payments without proper invoicing will be exposed to audits."

**Insight:** For small gyms collecting monthly memberships via SINPE (which is most of them in Costa Rica), this creates **hundreds of additional administrative entries per month**.

## SECTION 6: E-INVOICING BASELINE REQUIREMENT

### Already Mandatory for ALL Costa Rica Gym Owners

**Timeline:**
"The electronic invoice system was implemented gradually by sectors and economic activities, and concluded in 2018 with the obligation of the system for all taxpayers."

**Who Must Comply:**
"Applies to every Tax Obligee whether natural person, legal entity, dimex or nite."

This includes:
- Personal trainers operating as professionals
- Small independent gyms
- CrossFit boxes
- Large gym chains

### Requirements for Compliance

**Step 1:** Register as a taxpayer with Ministry of Finance (Hacienda)

**Step 2:** Register in the Virtual Tax Administration (ATV) system as an issuer-receiver

**Step 3:** Obtain a cryptographic key through ATV to digitally sign invoices

**Step 4:** Implement an electronic invoicing system that complies with current regulations

**Step 5:** Send each invoice to Hacienda for validation before customer acceptance

### The Technical Burden
"Taxpayers must issue and receive electronic receipts through the Ministry of Finance, which is responsible for validating each document before its acceptance."

**Insight:** Small gym owners without technical skills or software must either:
1. Pay for invoicing software (monthly cost)
2. Hire someone to manage it (labor cost)
3. Manually process every invoice through Hacienda portal (time cost)

**Sources:**
- [EDICOM - Costa Rica E-Invoicing](https://edicom.mx/blog/como-es-la-factura-electronica-en-costa-rica)
- [Factura Profesional FAQ](https://www.facturaprofesional.com/preguntas-frecuentes-factura-electronica)
- [Softland - Version 4.4 Changes](https://softland.com/cr/nuevos-cambios-de-la-facturacion-electronica-4-4/)

## SECTION 12: CULTURAL & BEHAVIORAL FACTORS

### SINPE Móvil Dominance in Costa Rica

**Context:** SINPE Móvil is the **primary payment method** for small businesses in Costa Rica.

**September 2025 Regulation Impact:**
"The Ministry of Finance has started requesting data from the Central Bank about users who receive more than 100 monthly transfers via SINPE Móvil, with the objective of identifying possible undeclared taxpayers."

**What This Means for Gyms:**
- A 50-member gym = 50 SINPE transfers per month (memberships only)
- Add class packages, personal training, retail = easily 80-120 transfers/month
- This puts them **above the 100-transfer audit threshold**

**Risk:** Gyms not properly invoicing SINPE payments are **high-priority audit targets**.

### Cash Culture Shift
Small gyms historically operated on:
- Cash payments
- Handwritten receipts
- Notebook tracking

**Now Required:**
- Digital payment tracking
- Real-time Hacienda reporting
- Cryptographic invoice signatures
- Cloud-based records

**Cultural Gap:** Many gym owners entered the business to **train people**, not become tax compliance experts.
